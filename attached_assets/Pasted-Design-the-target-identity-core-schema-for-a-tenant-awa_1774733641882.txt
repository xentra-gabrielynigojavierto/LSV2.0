Design the target identity/core schema for a tenant-aware, multi-organization, product-role platform.

Use this architecture as the source of truth:

1. Executive Summary
- Tenant is currently a monolithic actor with no internal organizational structure.
- Introduce Organization as the business actor inside a tenant.
- This unlocks cross-product roles, cross-tenant referrals/workflows, and party portals.

2. Domain Model
- Tenant = account boundary, billing unit, subdomain anchor. Never represents a specific business type.
- Organization = business actor inside a tenant (LAW_FIRM, PROVIDER, FUNDER, LIEN_OWNER, INTERNAL). Always owned by one tenant.
- Product = platform-wide capability bundle (CARECONNECT, SYNQLIEN, SYNQFUND).
- ProductRole = named participation right within a product workflow, tied to organization type.
- Capability = fine-grained permission within a ProductRole.
- Party = real-world individual (injured party, referred client) and is separate from User.

3. Authorization Model
- Authorization flow is: tenant authentication -> product access -> organization x product role -> capability check.
- Subdomain routing must remain tenant-based, not organization-based.
- Cross-tenant reads happen only through explicit workflow participation.

4. Product role examples
- CareConnect:
  - LAW_FIRM -> CARECONNECT_REFERRER
  - PROVIDER -> CARECONNECT_RECEIVER
- SynqLien:
  - PROVIDER -> SYNQLIEN_SELLER
  - LIEN_OWNER -> SYNQLIEN_BUYER
- SynqFund:
  - LAW_FIRM -> SYNQFUND_REFERRER
  - FUNDER -> SYNQFUND_FUNDER
  - injured party is a Party and later has a separate interface

5. Migration approach
- Non-destructive
- Backward compatible
- Feature-flag-gated
- Tenant-by-tenant rollout

Now design the target identity/core schema.

Requirements:
- keep Tenant separate from Organization
- Organization must reference TenantId
- support multiple organizations per tenant
- support one user belonging to multiple organizations in the future
- keep Users separate from Parties
- support product-specific roles such as:
  - CARECONNECT_REFERRER
  - CARECONNECT_RECEIVER
  - SYNQLIEN_SELLER
  - SYNQLIEN_BUYER
  - SYNQFUND_REFERRER
  - SYNQFUND_FUNDER
- support capability-based authorization
- support organization domains for subdomain and future custom domain routing
- support system roles and organization/product-scoped roles where needed
- preserve the idea that product access is separate from product role, and product role is separate from capability
- roles may exist for admin/UI convenience, but authorization must resolve through organization membership, product role, and capabilities
